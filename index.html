The pattern is consistent

High firing rate + high override rate = noise. Low firing rate + high action taken = signal. The data doesn't lie — but someone has to be watching it. That's what CDS governance is.

Two patients.
Same drug. Same ward. Same day.

Both just had a cardiac stent. Both prescribed clopidogrel — standard post-stent therapy. No CDS alert fires for either. Which drugs below do you think have known genomic variants that change how they work?

CDS alertNone fired
Patient B — Muthu s/o Rajan, 61M
Genomic result: not in system
PrescribedClopidogrel 75mg OD
Expected outcomeUnknown — no alert
CDS alertNone fired

Which of these drugs have known genomic variants that change how they work?

All of them.
Patient B is a CYP2C19 poor metaboliser.

🧬

Clopidogrel doesn't work for him — and no one knows.

Clopidogrel is a prodrug. It needs CYP2C19 to convert it into its active form. Poor metabolisers can't make that conversion. The drug sits inert. The stent stays unprotected. Without a PGx CDS alert, the prescriber has no idea.

What good PGx CDS looks like — NUHS implementation
⚠ Pharmacogenomics Warning: CYP2C19 POOR metaboliser
Patient may have significantly reduced platelet inhibition with clopidogrel due to impaired CYP2C19 metabolism.
